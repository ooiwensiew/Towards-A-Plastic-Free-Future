Action

Working Together for Change

Individual habits are important, but plastic pollution is a big problem that also needs teamwork.

When schools, families, neighbourhoods, and local organisations work together, we can create much stronger and longer-lasting change.

Join
or start

a community clean-up at a nearby park, river, or beach.

Participants can
collect rubbish,

separate recyclable plastic from other waste, and send recyclable items to recycling centres.

After the clean-up, the group can take photos of the collected rubbish and upload them on social media or paste them on notice boards to raise awareness.

This shows people how much waste ends up in the environment and encourages them to dispose of rubbish properly.

Encourage
local shops

or food stalls to reduce their plastic usage.

For
example,

politely approach stall owners and ask if they would consider offering a small discount for customers who bring their own containers.

When businesses see that customers care about sustainability, they are more likely to support these changes.

Schools
can run an

eco-bazaar or “thrift corner” where students bring gently used items such as books, stationery, or bags to swap or sell at low prices.

This reduces waste, gives items a second life, and encourages a culture of reusing rather than constant purchasing.

Share
information

through posters, social media, and community events to encourage more people to make responsible choices.

Raising awareness about plastic pollution helps the public understand how everyday habits contribute to environmental damage.
