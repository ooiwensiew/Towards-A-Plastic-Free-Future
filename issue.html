Plastic

Pollution Today

What is plastic pollution?

Plastic pollution happens when plastic waste is not managed properly and builds up in the environment.

Plastics

do not break down easily.
While it slowly breaks apart, it turns into tiny pieces called microplastics, which spread through soil, water, and even the air.

How does it affect nature?

In rivers and oceans, plastic waste traps and injures marine animals.

Turtles mistake plastic bags for jellyfish

Seabirds eat colourful plastic pieces

Fish get tangled in plastic nets

These animals can choke, starve, or be badly injured.

When
coral reefs

are covered with plastic, they become weaker and more likely to die.

This damages entire ecosystems that many species - including humans - depend on for food and income.

How does it affect us?

When
sea animals

eat microplastics, these particles move up the food chain and can end up on our plates.

In 2021, a study examined human cells exposed to contaminated drinking water, seafood, and table salt.

The results overwhelmingly showed that cells exposed to microplastics suffered from cytotoxicity (death of the cell), allergic reactions, and tissue damage.

The scientists also added that more research was needed as it is unclear how long microplastics remain in the human body and what that impact has.

Plastic
Waste

can also block drains and rivers, causing floods during heavy rain.

As floodwaters spread they can threaten lives, destroy belongings, damage vital infrastructure and prevent access to essential public services.
