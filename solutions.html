Small Habits, Big Impact

1

Bring Your Own Reusables

Carry a reusable water bottle, food container, and set of cutlery in your bag.

Use a cloth or canvas bag when shopping instead of plastic bags.

Use reusable straws and cups.

Over time, these small decisions prevent hundreds of single-use items from entering landfills and oceans.

2

Choose Products With Less Packaging

Buy
loose fruits

and vegetables instead of those packed in plastic trays.

Choose
large refill packs

instead of many small packets.
At home, you can refill existing bottles (such as soap or detergent) instead of buying new ones each time.

3

Practice the 3Rs

Reduce
Use fewer plastic items overall.

Reuse
Repurpose containers, jars, and bags.

Recycle
Sort plastics correctly so they can be processed.

Refusing unnecessary plastic is one of the simplest ways to cut down waste immediately.

4

Be a Smart Recycler

Recycling is only effective when it is done properly.

Learn
which types

of plastic your local recycling centre accepts, and make sure they are clean and dry before putting them into the recycling bin.

Mixing dirty containers or non-recyclable items into the bin can contaminate the batch and cause the whole bag to be thrown away.

When we
recycle correctly,

we help reduce the demand for new plastic and save energy and resources.
